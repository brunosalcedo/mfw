When It Rains It “Poors”: Poverty and Rainfall in Central Asia

(Job Market Paper)

This paper evaluates the causal relationship between rainfall and measures of poverty in central Asia using panel data. Using plausibly exogeneous variation in weather, I find that changes in rainfall predicts changes in the percentage of the population in poverty by between 0.2 and 0.5 standard deviations. This research implies that natural conditions result in large changes in material well-being.
